Movie Ticketing App

Initial Wireframes

Overview

The movie ticketing app is a UX portfolio project I embarked on after a project generator suggested making a movie ticketing app, where users can easily search for a movie showing close to them and buy a ticket. Seemed simple at first until I started researching existing apps and user pain points. Many users expressed frustration with clunky interfaces, poor filtering options, and lack of visual confirmations. My goal with this project was to create a seamless and enjoyable ticket purchasing experience that addresses these issues.

Conceptualization

I kicked off this project by conducting extensive user research and competitive analysis online. I went to social media sites where moviegoers frequent such as Reddit (and related subreddits), Twitter, Bluesky, and various online forms I came across during my research. I found that users were consistenly frustrated with four distinct things:

  1. Seat mapping: Moviegoers had a hard time navigating through traditional seat maps, struggling to find the “best” available seats during peak hours or assigning group seats. Sometimes users would even resort to keeping spreadsheets of their favorite seats before booking a ticket.
    2. I can usually tell the general layout just by looking at it but the seat maps can be deceiving sometimes
    - anaccount50, r/AMCsAList
  2. Search Filtering: Users had to navigate through movies before seeing what is available near them, when some moviegoers preferred wanting to see what is near them or at certain theaters.
  3. Relevant Options Casual moviegoers were wanting options that allowed them to see movies that are personalized to their interest instead of sorting through latest blockbusters.
  4. Poor Confirmation Users were confused on whether or not they had even purchased tickets, with a lack of proper confirmation elements.

Wireframing
